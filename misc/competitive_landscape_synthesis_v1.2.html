Competitive landscape — synthesis.

Restaurant forecasting is no longer a point-solution space. In an eight-week window late 2025, six competitors shipped agentic AI features, including the two POS incumbents Kirra had positioned as partners. This report synthesises 14 deep-dives into a directional verdict, ranked threats, and a 90-day strategic posture.

§ 04 The #1 threat · Tier 1 Severe

Nory.

The threat that should restructure Kirra's fundraising narrative. Capital-stack advantage + founder-operator origin + US beachhead live.

Nory is the only competitor in the field that matches Kirra on capital, breadth, founder-market fit, and a US restaurant beachhead simultaneously. Their product surface is fundamentally agentic, not dashboard-anchored, and Kinnevik-led capital gives them runway to outspend on US growth through 2027.

The directional response is clear: do not try to match Nory's module count. Kirra wins on depth and outcome verification, not breadth. Compete on the per-forecast 48-hour reward signal that Nory's 95–98% marketing claim conspicuously avoids architecting.

Watch for two events: a per-forecast accuracy dashboard exposed to operators, or $5M US ARR within six months of the March 2026 launch. Either collapses Kirra's window to lock juice / ramen / breakfast / fast casual reference customers.

§ 05 The reframe · Toast IQ + Square AI · Oct 2025

The "Toast as partner" framing is broken.

Both POS incumbents now ship native conversational forecasting bundled free. The v2 map's partnership posture must be retired this quarter.

Toast IQ — Oct 2025
  • ~148K locations. Bundled-free conversational AI sitting natively inside the POS — zero marginal cost to existing Toast customers.
  • Forecasting, menu intelligence, agentic write-back. Workflow surface deeper than any standalone competitor at the same price point ($0).
  • Distribution moat is the threat, not the feature. Even if Toast IQ accuracy lags Kirra by 10pp, the bundled-free + zero-onboarding posture wins against unsophisticated operators.
  • Mitigation: reframe Toast as a distribution channel where Kirra beats Toast IQ on outcome for operators with >$1.5M/loc revenue. Interview 5 Toast customers using Toast IQ this quarter.
Square AI / Managerbot — Oct 2025
  • Mirror threat on the Square half of Kirra's POS split. Same bundled-free posture, same agentic write-back surface.
  • Strongest food-native signal disclosure in the field. Explicit weather + events + news + reviews signals — narrows Kirra's signal-stack moat materially.
  • Proactive agent surface. Pushes recommendations to sellers; not a passive dashboard.
  • Mitigation: Kirra's Square integration must be architected so calibrated accuracy beats Managerbot on day one of onboarding. If that is not true, the Square half of the ICP is at risk.
Toast IQ accuracy lagging Kirra by 10pp does not save the partnership. Bundled-free + zero-onboarding wins against any operator who has not yet been taught what verified accuracy means.

Kirra's answer is per-customer reward signal, not corpus size.

§ 11 Strategic implications · Positioning, pricing, sequencing, partnerships

What this landscape changes.

Eight shifts to make this quarter. Each is anchored in a specific competitive movement, not a general posture change.

Positioning

Stop leading with "AI demand forecasting" — every competitor claims this now. Lead with "the only forecasting platform that logs every prediction against POS actuals within 48 hours and publishes per-customer accuracy." That sentence is Kirra's only durable claim in the current market.

Pricing

The $150–250/loc/mo base is now a pincer (Toast IQ + Square AI free; Lineup at $79; R365 Core at $249 bundles accounting). Move the performance tier (originally Year 2) forward. Pilot priced low-base + high-performance-fee monetises the outcome loop directly and inverts the bundled-free threat.

Sequencing

Build the outcome-loop visibility surface before expanding breadth. An operator-visible "forecast vs actual" dashboard exposing per-location MAPE, calibration drift, and override acceptance is the demo every sales call needs. Inventory and procurement depth should wait until reference customers exist. Depth earns the right to width.

Fundraising narrative

Nory's $37M Series B sets the ceiling for what AI-native restaurant forecasting raises look like in 2026. Frame Kirra's moat as time-locked data infrastructure that is impossible to replicate from a standing start — explicitly name the 48h loop, override pattern library, and recipe-to-ingredient mapping as three compounding assets activating in sequence. Do not pitch breadth.

Toast partnership posture

Toast IQ is getting better fast. Question: can Kirra be a premium layer above Toast IQ for operators who have outgrown its accuracy, or is Toast IQ a substitute? Interview 5 Toast customers using Toast IQ this quarter. Until then: assume substitute for unsophisticated operators, complement for >$1.5M/loc operators who care about 2% food cost.

Square partnership posture

Square AI Managerbot is a bigger near-term threat than Toast IQ — stronger explicit food-native signal stack. Kirra's Square integration must be architected so calibrated accuracy beats Managerbot on day one of onboarding. If that is not true, the Square half of the ICP is at risk.

Year 1 customer choice

Select reference customers where the outcome loop is most visible: high-perishability concepts (juice, ramen, breakfast, fast casual) at 3–15 locations on Square or Toast. Avoid customers currently on MarginEdge, R365 or Crunchtime — displacement sales cycle is longer than verification cycle. Zero undetected accuracy degradations is the right Year 1 goal.

D2C posture

D2C forecaster stack (Prediko, Cogsy, Fabrikatör) is crowded, cheap, generalist. Kirra's $1,000–$2,000/brand/mo is 2–4× the competitive band. Do not enter D2C until restaurant outcome loop has produced verified case studies. Then enter with food-native + KeHE/UNFI + verified outcome as a single premium pitch. Year 2 timing is correct.
